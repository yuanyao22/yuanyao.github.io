I am a postdoctoral scholar in the Department of Environmental Health Sciences at the UCLA Fielding School of Public Health. I received my doctoral degree in environment and health from Peking University.

My research focuses on the intersection of air pollution, climate change, and human health, with an emphasis on wildfire smoke exposure, non-exhaust emissions, and community-engaged environmental health research. I use advanced field monitoring and exposure science methods to understand how air pollution affects communities, particularly during climate-related disasters. My work aims to translate scientific evidence into practical solutions that reduce health disparities, strengthen public health preparedness, and support equitable energy transitions.
